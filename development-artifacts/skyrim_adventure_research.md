# Skyrim Adventure Research

## Lore Overview

Skyrim is the northernmost province of Tamriel, a harsh and frozen land of towering mountains, ancient Nordic ruins, and fierce warrior culture. Home to the Nords, Skyrim is divided into nine holds, each governed by a jarl. The province is defined by its Viking-inspired culture, reverence for the Thu'um (Voice), worship of the Nine Divines (particularly Talos), and a long history of conflict spanning the Dragon War, the Great War, and the Stormcloak Rebellion.

The events of TES V: Skyrim (4E 201) define the province's most recent era: the return of Alduin and the dragons, the Stormcloak Civil War, the machinations of the Thalmor, and numerous guild questlines including the Companions, Thieves Guild, Dark Brotherhood, and College of Winterhold.

## Races & Peoples

- **Nords** — Dominant race, warrior culture, reverence for Ysgramor and Sovngarde, mastery of the Thu'um among select few
- **Imperials** — Significant military/political presence via the Empire and Imperial Legion
- **Dunmer** — Refugee community in Windhelm's Gray Quarter after the Red Year
- **Argonians** — Dockworker communities, marginalized in cities
- **Bretons/Reachmen** — Native Reach population, including the Forsworn insurgency
- **Khajiit** — Trade caravans outside city walls
- **Altmer/Thalmor** — Political operatives enforcing the White-Gold Concordat

## Adventure Proposals

### Proposal 1: Shadows Over Riften (Thieves Guild / Nightingales)

**Attributes: WIL/AGI** (unused pair)

#### Adventure Header
- **Name:** Shadows Over Riften
- **Setting:** Riften -> Ratway -> Goldenglow Estate -> Irkngthand -> Twilight Sepulcher
- **Storyline:** The Thieves Guild is in decline, its luck stolen along with the Skeleton Key from the Twilight Sepulcher. As you rise through the Guild's ranks, you discover that Guildmaster Mercer Frey has betrayed the Nightingales and Nocturnal herself. Recover the Skeleton Key and restore the Guild's fortune.
- **Difficulty:** 2

#### Deck Design
- **Archetype:** WIL/AGI Pilfer Synergy ("Nightingale" deck)
- **Core Cards:**
  - **Brynjolf** (AGI 5c 4/5, legendary) — Drain + magicka on Pilfer/Drain
  - **The Gray Fox** (AGI 5c 4/4, legendary) — Pilfer: reveal + Summon: steal from discard
  - **Master of Thieves** (WIL/AGI 4c 3/5, epic) — Pilfer creatures attack extra time
  - **Thieves' Den** (AGI 4c support, legendary) — All creatures get Pilfer: +1/+1
  - **Thieves Guild Recruit** (AGI 1c 1/2) — Pilfer: draw card
  - **Thieves Guild Fence** — Pilfer synergy
  - **Dune Smuggler** (AGI 3c 3/1) — Pilfer: move + cover synergy
  - **Descendant of Alkosh** (WIL, legendary) — Pilfer: +1/+1 + random keyword
  - **Quin'rawl Burglar** (AGI 6c, legendary) — Drain + Pilfer: +4/+4
  - **Helstrom Footpad** (AGI 2c 2/2) — cheap Pilfer enabler
  - **Hive Defender** (WIL 4c 3/6 Guard) — defensive backbone
  - **Kvatch Soldier** (WIL 2c 2/3 Guard) — early defense
  - **Execute** (WIL 1c removal) — efficient removal
  - **The Ultimate Heist** (AGI 12c action) — Pilfer cost reduction, rune-breaking finisher
- **Legendary Anchors:** Brynjolf, The Gray Fox, Thieves' Den, Master of Thieves
- **Mechanical Coherence:** Dense Pilfer synergy creates a snowball engine. Small creatures Pilfer for buffs and card advantage while Master of Thieves + Thieves' Den amplify every attack. Brynjolf provides magicka acceleration. WIL contributes Guard creatures for defense and Execute/Javelin for removal.

#### Boss & Enemies
- **Final Boss: Mercer Frey** — Custom enemy deck (Breton rogue, invisible/elusive mechanics). He stole the Skeleton Key and uses it to break into Irkngthand's vault. Boss gimmick: "Mercer has the Skeleton Key. At the start of his turn, a random friendly creature gains +0/+2 and Guard." (Thematically: he unlocks potential/defenses.)
- **Mini-boss: Dwarven Centurion** — Mercer leads you into the Dwemer ruin Irkngthand; a Dwemer Centurion guards the inner chambers. Enemy deck: Dwemer automaton theme (25 Dwemer cards available, NEU).
- **Enemy deck themes:**
  - Corrupt guild members / rival thieves (AGI-based Pilfer enemies that steal your resources)
  - Dwemer automatons in Irkngthand (NEU Dwemer — Spider Worker, Dwarven Sphere, Dwarven Ballista)
- **Enemy races:** Breton, Khajiit (rival thieves), Dwemer (automatons)

#### Adventure Nodes
- **Boons:** "Nocturnal's Favor", "Luck of the Guild"
- **Events:** "The Goldenglow Job" — A choice: sneak past the mercenaries (gain cover for all creatures next combat) or burn the bee hives (deal 3 damage to all enemies at the start of next combat, but lose 5 max HP from a fire trap)
- **Shop/Reinforcement:** "The Ragged Flagon" (shop), "Nightingale Recruit" (reinforcement)
- **Augment nodes:** "Nocturnal's Blessing" (creature augment — shadow/stealth theme), "Pickpocket's Haul" (action augment — stolen goods)

#### Feasibility Rating
- **Card pool depth:** Excellent — 45+ Pilfer cards + WIL Guard staples
- **Boss availability:** Custom only (Mercer Frey not in catalog) — straightforward to build an AGI/INT enemy deck
- **Lore richness:** Deep — full TES V questline with betrayal, ancient ruins, and Daedric intrigue
- **Mechanical uniqueness:** High — only Pilfer-centric adventure; plays as tempo/snowball vs the existing aggro/control/magic decks
- **Suggested difficulty:** 2

### Proposal 2: Tyranny of the Sun (Dawnguard)

**Attributes: WIL/END** (unused pair)

#### Adventure Header
- **Name:** Tyranny of the Sun
- **Setting:** Fort Dawnguard -> Dimhollow Crypt -> Soul Cairn -> Castle Volkihar
- **Storyline:** The Volkihar vampires have awakened from their ancient slumber. Lord Harkon seeks Auriel's Bow to blot out the sun and unleash an eternal night. Join the Dawnguard, rally holy warriors and armored trolls, and storm Castle Volkihar to end the vampire menace before darkness swallows Skyrim.
- **Difficulty:** 3

#### Deck Design
- **Archetype:** WIL/END Holy Warriors / Anti-Undead ("Dawnguard" deck)
- **Core Cards:**
  - **General Tullius** (WIL/END 6c 2/2, legendary) — Summon: Guards in each lane + death scaling
  - **Armored Troll** (WIL/END 5c 3/7, epic, DG set) — Regenerate, immune to damage on own turn
  - **Dawnbreaker** (WIL item, legendary) — Slay: banish undead
  - **Penitus Oculatus Envoy** (WIL 2c 2/2, DG set) — Summon: immune to Lethal
  - **The End of Vampires** (WIL 7c action, DG set) — Costs less per enemy undead, banishes a creature
  - **Hive Defender** (WIL 4c 3/6 Guard) — defensive stalwart
  - **Piercing Javelin** (WIL 5c removal) — hard removal
  - **Imperial Legionnaire** (WIL 3c 3/4 Imperial) — solid body
  - **Legion Praefect** (WIL 7c 4/4 Imperial) — Summon: +1/+1 to all friendly
  - **Night Shadow** (END 6c, epic) — Breakthrough + Drain
  - **Cave Bear** (END 5c 5/6) — solid midrange
  - **Tree Minder** (END 1c 1/1) — Guard, early defense
  - **Frostbite Spider** (END 3c 3/3 Lethal) — removal + tempo
  - **Haunting Spirit** (END 3c 3/3) — Last Gasp: buff a friendly creature
- **Legendary Anchors:** General Tullius, Dawnbreaker, Armored Troll
- **Mechanical Coherence:** WIL provides an army of Guard creatures and Imperial tokens (Tullius summons Colovian Troopers). END provides resilience with Regenerate (Armored Troll), Drain (Night Shadow), and Lethal (Frostbite Spider). Dawnbreaker and The End of Vampires are flavorful tech against vampire enemies. The deck plays as a midrange control shell — build a wall of Guards, stabilize with Drain/healing, and outvalue with Tullius scaling.

#### Boss & Enemies
- **Final Boss: Lord Harkon** (END 4c 2/4 legendary, transforms to Harkon Vampire Lord 6/6 Drain + Slay: summon Bat). Boss gimmick: Starts as 2/4, but if he slays a creature he transforms into the 6/6 Vampire Lord and begins snowballing with Bat tokens.
- **Mini-boss: Vampire Nightlord** — A powerful vampire lieutenant (6c 5/6 Drain + Guard). Enemy deck: Vampire/undead theme.
- **Enemy deck themes:**
  - Volkihar vampires (20 vampire cards: Lord Harkon, Nightlord Vampire, Vampire Fledgling, Barrow Stalker, Doomcrag Vampire, Night Talon Lord, Blood Magic Lord, Death Hound + Skeleton/Spirit padding)
  - Soul Cairn undead (Skeleton/Spirit cards — 63 combined)
- **Enemy races:** Vampire, Skeleton, Spirit, Beast (Bats, Death Hounds)

#### Adventure Nodes
- **Boons:** "Blessing of Stendarr", "Dawnguard Oath"
- **Events:** "Serana's Plea" — A vampire princess begs for help escaping her father. Choice: Trust her (gain a powerful Drain creature card for your deck, but lose 5 max HP from her vampiric aura) or Reject her (keep full HP, gain 25 gold)
- **Shop/Reinforcement:** "Gunmar's Forge" (shop — equipment/troll focus), "Dawnguard Recruit" (reinforcement)
- **Augment nodes:** "Runed Crossbow" (creature augment — holy weapon theme), "Scroll of Sun Fire" (action augment — anti-undead magic)

#### Feasibility Rating
- **Card pool depth:** Good — WIL Imperials (20+) + END creatures + DG set cards. Player deck is solid; enemy vampire pool is 20 cards.
- **Boss availability:** Ready — Lord Harkon is a legendary with built-in transformation mechanic, perfect for a boss fight
- **Lore richness:** Deep — Dawnguard is one of Skyrim's most popular DLCs, with a compelling villain, a beloved NPC (Serana), and memorable locations
- **Mechanical uniqueness:** High — anti-undead tech cards (Dawnbreaker, The End of Vampires) give the deck a unique identity; Guard-heavy WIL/END plays differently from all existing decks
- **Suggested difficulty:** 3

### Proposal 3: The Jagged Crown (Civil War — Stormcloak Side)

**Attributes: STR/END** (unused pair)

#### Adventure Header
- **Name:** The Jagged Crown
- **Setting:** Windhelm -> Korvanjund -> Whiterun -> Solitude
- **Storyline:** Skyrim burns with civil war. Ulfric Stormcloak has slain the High King and claimed Skyrim's independence. Rally the Nord warriors of Eastmarch, recover the legendary Jagged Crown from the ancient ruins of Korvanjund, and march on Solitude to end Imperial rule in Skyrim once and for all.
- **Difficulty:** 2

#### Deck Design
- **Archetype:** STR/END Nord Rally / Warrior ("Stormcloak" deck)
- **Core Cards:**
  - **Ulfric Stormcloak** (STR 5c 6/5, legendary) — Breakthrough + Shout: draw Nord
  - **Unrelenting Force** (STR 3c Shout action) — Unsummon, synergizes with Ulfric
  - **Ulfric's Zealot** (STR 4c 5/2) — Last Gasp: Heavy Battleaxe to hand
  - **Ulfric's Housecarl** (STR/WIL 4c 3/3) — enemy rune destroyed: draw card — wait, this is STR/WIL dual. Can still be used in STR/END deck? No, dual attribute cards need BOTH attributes to be in the deck. Since the deck is STR/END, STR/WIL duals wouldn't be available.

  Let me reconsider. STR/END can use STR cards, END cards, NEU cards, and STR/END dual cards.

  - **Ulfric Stormcloak** (STR 5c 6/5, legendary) — Breakthrough + Shout: draw Nord
  - **Unrelenting Force** (STR 3c Shout action) — Unsummon
  - **Ulfric's Zealot** (STR 4c 5/2) — Last Gasp: Heavy Battleaxe
  - **Nord Firebrand** (STR 0c 1/1 Charge) — cheap aggro
  - **Battlerage Orc** (STR 3c 2/3) — Charge
  - **Fearless Northlander** (STR 3c 4/2 Breakthrough) — damage-triggered buff
  - **Graystone Ravager** (STR 4c 4/3) — solid body
  - **Rampaging Minotaur** (STR 5c 5/4 Breakthrough) — push damage
  - **Morthal Watchman** (STR 3c 3/3) — Nord body
  - **Skaven Pyromancer** (STR 3c 2/2) — Summon: 1 damage to lane
  - **Night Shadow** (END 6c Breakthrough + Drain) — midrange finisher
  - **Deadly Draugr** (END 1c 1/1 Lethal) — cheap removal
  - **Frostbite Spider** (END 3c 3/3 Lethal) — efficient removal
  - **Haunting Spirit** (END 3c 3/3) — Last Gasp: buff
  - **Bone Colossus** (END 9c 5/10 legendary) — top end, summon skeletons
- **Legendary Anchors:** Ulfric Stormcloak, Bone Colossus (or another END legendary like Night Predator)
- **Mechanical Coherence:** STR provides aggressive Nord warriors with Breakthrough and Charge. END provides staying power with Drain, Lethal, and resilient bodies. Ulfric + Shout cards create a draw engine that keeps the aggression flowing. The deck plays as a midrange aggro deck — push damage with Breakthrough creatures, use Lethal to remove blockers, and finish with Drain to stay alive.

#### Boss & Enemies
- **Final Boss: General Tullius** (WIL/END 6c 2/2 legendary, summons Guards + scales on death triggers). Boss gimmick: Tullius floods the board with Imperial soldiers (Colovian Troopers with Guard) and grows stronger as they fall. The player must punch through the wall of Guards to reach him.
- **Mini-boss: Imperial Captain** — A mid-rank Imperial officer. Enemy deck: Imperial/Willpower soldiers.
- **Enemy deck themes:**
  - Imperial Legion soldiers (WIL Imperial cards: Imperial Legionnaire, Kvatch Soldier, Legion Praefect, Imperial Reinforcements, Bruma Profiteer, Hero of Anvil + Guard cards)
  - Thalmor agents supporting the Empire (INT High Elf cards — can reuse Thalmor Agent enemy deck)
- **Enemy races:** Imperial (WIL), High Elf (INT)

#### Adventure Nodes
- **Boons:** "Talos's Blessing", "War Cry of the North"
- **Events:** "The Jarl's Ultimatum" — An allied jarl wavers in his support. Choice: Threaten him with the Voice (gain +2/+0 to all creatures this run, but lose 10 max HP from the political fallout) or Negotiate peacefully (gain a random Nord recruit)
- **Shop/Reinforcement:** "Windhelm War Quarters" (shop), "Stormcloak Camp" (reinforcement)
- **Augment nodes:** "Skyforge Weapons" (creature augment — Nordic weapon theme), "Battle Standard" (action augment — rally/morale)

#### Feasibility Rating
- **Card pool depth:** Good — 86 Nord cards (STR-heavy) + END creatures provide deep pool. Stormcloak-specific cards (10) need Nord padding but are flavorful.
- **Boss availability:** Ready — General Tullius is a legendary with unique token-generation + scaling mechanic
- **Lore richness:** Deep — the Civil War is the central political conflict of TES V, instantly recognizable
- **Mechanical uniqueness:** Moderate — STR/END midrange aggro is a common archetype, but Ulfric's Shout synergy and the Civil War theme differentiate it. The Imperial enemy decks with Guard-heavy boards create a distinct challenge.
- **Suggested difficulty:** 2

### Proposal 4: Forbidden Knowledge (Dragonborn DLC / Solstheim)

**Attributes: STR/INT** (unused pair)

#### Adventure Header
- **Name:** Forbidden Knowledge
- **Setting:** Raven Rock (Solstheim) -> Tel Mithryn -> Apocrypha -> Summit of Apocrypha
- **Storyline:** On the ash-covered island of Solstheim, a dark influence has taken root. The first Dragonborn, Miraak, reaches across the veil of Apocrypha to enslave the island's people. Delve into the Black Books of Hermaeus Mora, navigate the twisted realm of forbidden knowledge, and confront Miraak before he breaks free from his Daedric prison to dominate Tamriel.
- **Difficulty:** 3

#### Deck Design
- **Archetype:** STR/INT Battlemage — Item Synergy + Spell Damage ("Solstheim Expedition" deck)
- **Core Cards:**
  - **Nahkriin, Dragon Priest** (INT 9c 5/5, legendary) — Summon: draw card, reduce cost to 0
  - **Wildfire Dragon** (STR 7c 6/6, legendary) — Breakthrough + Summon: 1 damage to all + Slay: +1/+1
  - **Lightning Bolt** (INT 4c action) — 4 damage, Prophecy
  - **Firebolt** (INT 2c action) — 2 damage
  - **Fire Storm** (INT 6c action) — 3 damage to all enemies
  - **Ice Spike** (INT 2c action) — 2 damage, draw card
  - **Crystal Tower Crafter** (INT 1c 1/1) — gains +1/+1 on action play
  - **Telvanni Arcanist** (INT 3c 3/2) — Last Gasp: random action to hand
  - **Breton Conjurer** (INT 5c 1/1 Ward) — Ward broken: summon 5/5 Frost Atronach
  - **Nord Firebrand** (STR 0c 1/1 Charge) — cheap creature
  - **Skaven Pyromancer** (STR 3c 2/2) — Summon: 1 damage to lane
  - **Battlerage Orc** (STR 3c 2/3 Charge) — aggression
  - **Rampaging Minotaur** (STR 5c 5/4 Breakthrough) — threat
  - **Rapid Shot** (STR 1c action) — 1 damage, draws on kill
- **Legendary Anchors:** Nahkriin Dragon Priest, Wildfire Dragon
- **Mechanical Coherence:** INT provides action-heavy spell damage and card generation (Crystal Tower Crafter grows with each spell). STR provides aggressive creatures and Breakthrough to push damage. The deck plays a spell-slinger/tempo style — clear the board with burn spells, grow Crystal Tower Crafter, then drop large threats to finish.

#### Boss & Enemies
- **Final Boss: Miraak** — Custom enemy deck (since Miraak the card is already in the Dragon Crisis player deck, the boss version would be an enemy-only enhanced version). Boss gimmick: "At the start of Miraak's turn, he steals a random creature from your lane." (Reflects his Bend Will shout and mind control of Solstheim's people.)
- **Mini-boss: Dragon Priest** — Ancient Draugr priest with powerful magical attacks. Enemy deck: Skeleton/Dragon Priest themed (Nahkriin, Dragon Cult Ghost, Draugr cards).
- **Enemy deck themes:**
  - Seekers and Lurkers of Apocrypha (Daedra subtype cards — 73 available)
  - Cultists and Dragon Priests (Skeleton/Spirit + Dragon cards for Miraak's forces)
- **Enemy races:** Daedra (Apocrypha denizens), Skeleton (cultists/draugr), Dragon (Miraak's enslaved dragons)

#### Adventure Nodes
- **Boons:** "Hermaeus Mora's Insight", "Black Book Power"
- **Events:** "The Black Book" — You discover a Black Book radiating dark power. Choice: Read it (gain a powerful action card, but Hermaeus Mora takes 10 max HP as payment for the knowledge) or Destroy it (gain 50 gold from selling the remains, keep full HP)
- **Shop/Reinforcement:** "Raven Rock Market" (shop), "Skaal Village Allies" (reinforcement)
- **Augment nodes:** "Hermaeus Mora's Gift" (creature augment — tentacle/forbidden power theme), "Spell Inscription" (action augment — arcane knowledge)

#### Feasibility Rating
- **Card pool depth:** Good — INT action cards are abundant (45+), STR creatures are deep. Battlemage archetype is well-supported.
- **Boss availability:** Needs custom — Miraak card exists but is in the Dragon Crisis deck; boss would be enemy-only with a unique "steal creature" mechanic
- **Lore richness:** Deep — Dragonborn DLC is one of Skyrim's most memorable expansions, Miraak is an iconic villain, and Apocrypha is visually/thematically distinctive
- **Mechanical uniqueness:** High — only spell-slinger/burn deck in the adventure lineup; Crystal Tower Crafter + action spam is a completely different play pattern
- **Suggested difficulty:** 3

## Recommendation

**Build first: Tyranny of the Sun (Dawnguard)**

Rationale:
1. **Boss is ready** — Lord Harkon exists as a legendary with a built-in transformation mechanic that creates a naturally escalating boss fight
2. **Thematic richness** — Dawnguard is one of Skyrim's most beloved DLCs; vampires vs holy warriors is a classic fantasy hook
3. **Mechanical uniqueness** — WIL/END Guard-heavy anti-undead is unlike any existing deck; Dawnbreaker and The End of Vampires provide flavorful anti-vampire tech
4. **Enemy pool depth** — 20 vampire cards + 63 undead cards = rich enemy deck variety
5. **Unused pair** — WIL/END hasn't been used in any adventure
