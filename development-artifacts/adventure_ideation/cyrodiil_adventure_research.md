# Cyrodiil Adventure Research

Research for designing Path of Tamriel adventures in the Cyrodiil region.

---

## Lore Overview

Cyrodiil is the central province of Tamriel, known as "the Heartland." It is the seat of the Empire, home to the Imperials, and the setting of TES IV: Oblivion. The province ranges from the fertile Nibenay Valley in the east to the rugged Colovian Highlands in the west, with the Imperial City — built atop the ancient Ayleid White-Gold Tower — at its center. Cyrodiil has been the epicenter of Tamriel's most consequential events: the Alessian Slave Rebellion, the rise and fall of multiple empires, the Oblivion Crisis, and the Great War against the Aldmeri Dominion.

## Races & Peoples

- **Imperials** — dominant race. Skilled diplomats, administrators, and soldiers. Known for discipline and heavy infantry tactics
- **Ayleids (extinct)** — ancient "Wild Elves" who built the White-Gold Tower and enslaved humans. Practiced star-worship and dark magic. Ruins throughout Cyrodiil
- **Bretons/Nords/other races** — significant minorities in border cities (Bruma has Nord population, etc.)
- **Daedra** — major enemies during the Oblivion Crisis, and during ESO Blackwood (Mehrunes Dagon plots)

### Notable Creatures (Enemy Deck Material)
- **Daedra** — Dremora, Scamps, Xivilai, Daedroths, Atronachs (huge card pool: 74 cards with "Daedra" subtype)
- **Undead** — Zombies, Skeletons, Liches, Ghosts (Ayleid ruin inhabitants)
- **Minotaurs** — native to Cyrodiil's forests
- **Goblins** — common enemies in wilderness and ruins

## Signature Cultural Elements

### The Imperial Legion
Cyrodiil's defining military identity. Heavy infantry, disciplined ranks, token generation (Imperial Grunts, Colovian Troopers). The WIL attribute is packed with Imperial Legion cards that summon tokens and buff each other.

### The Arena
The Imperial City's Arena is an iconic location. Gladiatorial combat, rising through ranks. Cards: Gladiator Arena (support), Lord of the Arena.

### The Oblivion Crisis
TES IV's main storyline. Mehrunes Dagon's invasion via Oblivion Gates, the Mythic Dawn cult, and Martin Septim's sacrifice. Strong card support through the Jaws of Oblivion set: Invade mechanic, Oblivion Gates, Daedra cards, Mankar Camoran.

### Ayleid Heritage
Ancient ruins scattered across Cyrodiil. Star-worship, Welkynd/Varla stones, enslaved humanity. Umaril the Unfeathered represents this legacy.

### Guild Culture
Every major guild is headquartered in Cyrodiil — Fighters Guild, Mages Guild, Thieves Guild, Dark Brotherhood. Rich storyline material.

## Adventure Theme Proposals

### 1. "The Gates of Oblivion" (Oblivion Crisis — Imperial City / Kvatch / Bruma)

**Setting**: Kvatch ruins -> the Oblivion Gate -> Chorrol countryside -> Battle of Bruma -> Imperial City -> Mankar Camoran's Paradise

**Storyline**: The Mythic Dawn has assassinated the Emperor and opened Oblivion Gates across Cyrodiil. The player must close gates, rally the Legion, and ultimately confront Mankar Camoran in his Paradise realm to recover the Amulet of Kings before Mehrunes Dagon can manifest.

#### Enemy Decks

#### 1. Kvatch Survivors (Combat)
- **Focus:** `wil_kvatch_soldier` *Kvatch Soldier* (WIL, 2 cost, 2/3 Guard) — Guard body holding the broken gate.
- **Supporting:** `wil_imperial_legionnaire` *Imperial Legionnaire*, `wil_septim_guardsman` *Septim Guardsman*, `wil_scouting_patrol` *Scouting Patrol*, `end_colovian_trooper` *Colovian Trooper*
- **Modifier:** *New mechanic:* The lane nearest the city begins with a smouldering Kvatch token — friendly Guards there gain +0/+2.

#### 2. Scamp Swarm at the Gate (Combat)
- **Focus:** `joo_wil_bedlam` *Bedlam* (WIL, 1 cost action) — Floods the enemy hand with 1/1 Scamps.
- **Supporting:** `wil_scamp` *Scamp*, `joo_str_invasion_scout` *Invasion Scout*, `joo_int_invasion_vanguard` *Invasion Vanguard*, `joo_wil_clannfear_runt` *Clannfear Runt*
- **Modifier:** *New mechanic:* Every 2 turns, an Oblivion Gate opens in a random lane summoning a 1/1 Scamp.

#### 3. Mythic Dawn Ambush (Combat)
- **Focus:** `joo_str_agent_of_mehrunes_dagon` *Agent of Mehrunes Dagon* (STR, 2 cost, 3/2) — Grows each time the enemy Invades.
- **Supporting:** `joo_int_mythic_dawn_informer` *Mythic Dawn Informer*, `joo_wil_mythic_dawn_zealot` *Mythic Dawn Zealot*, `joo_int_mythic_dawn_acolyte` *Mythic Dawn Acolyte*, `joo_neu_oblivion_invasion` *Oblivion Invasion*

- **Modifier:** The Paradise — both players start with an Oblivion Gate already placed in each lane on the enemy side. Player cannot target enemy Gates directly with actions.

**Node highlights**:
- Boon: "Blessing of Akatosh"
- Event: "The Fall of Kvatch" — risk entering the burning city (take 5 damage, gain a powerful recruit) or go around
- Event: "Martin's Plea" — the heir asks for help (sacrifice a card to gain Martin Septim's buff for all creatures)
- Shop: "Imperial City Market District"
- Creature augment: "Legion Forge"
- Mini-boss: Martin Septim (corrupted banked-magicka duel)

**Feasibility**: Excellent — Jaws of Oblivion set directly supplies the Invade/Gate/Daedra enemy pool, plus Mankar Camoran and Martin Septim as ready-made legendary bosses.

### 3. "Grand Champion" (Arena Combat — Imperial City Arena)

**Setting**: Imperial City Arena district -> qualifying rounds -> champion's quarters -> the Arena floor -> Grand Champion confrontation

**Storyline**: The player enters the Imperial Arena to prove their worth. Each match is a gladiatorial bout with escalating difficulty. Between fights, the player can visit the Bloodworks for reinforcements or make deals with shady sponsors. The final challenge: the reigning Grand Champion and the mysterious Lord of the Arena.

#### Enemy Decks

#### 1. Yellow Team Initiates (Combat)
- **Focus:** `wil_imperial_legionnaire` *Imperial Legionnaire* (WIL, 3 cost 3/4) — Vanilla grunt combatant.
- **Supporting:** `wil_kvatch_soldier` *Kvatch Soldier*, `wil_skingrad_patroller` *Skingrad Patroller*, `str_morthal_watchman` *Morthal Watchman*, `end_colovian_trooper` *Colovian Trooper*
- **Modifier:** Creatures may only be summoned to the lane currently "in the ring" (alternates each turn).

#### 2. Beast Pit (Combat)
- **Focus:** `wil_pit_lion` *Pit Lion* (WIL, 3 cost epic 5/5) — Requires a creature in each lane.

## Recommendation

**Build "The Gates of Oblivion" first.** It has the strongest combination across all criteria for an enemy-deck-driven adventure:

1. **Enemy pool depth** — Jaws of Oblivion is explicitly designed around the Invade/Daedra/Gate theme. The 10 encounters easily stretch across early Scamp swarms, mid-tier Dremora warbands, and late-game Keeper/Forces of Destruction fights without repeating focus cards.

2. **Boss quality** — Mankar Camoran's "Invade after every action + Gates have +4/+0" plus the Paradise modifier (Gates pre-placed, player can't target them directly) creates a genuine race condition. Martin Septim's mini-boss transformation countdown is a natural thematic lead-in.

3. **Lore iconicness** — The Oblivion Crisis is arguably the most recognizable TES storyline after Skyrim's dragon crisis. Kvatch's fall, Oblivion Gates, Martin's sacrifice, Paradise — all deeply embedded in player memory.

4. **Mechanical variety** — The 10 encounter modifiers mix lane effects (Kvatch soldier's buff, Sigillum Sanguis damage), enemy keyword grants (Daedra Guard while Gate exists), scripted events (Siege Crawler on turn 6), and the Paradise pre-placement twist — giving designers a clear template for later provinces.

**Second priority**: "The King of Worms" — Mannimarco's discard-summoning boss mechanic is one of the most unique in the catalog, and the necromancy theme supplies nine varied encounters from Apprentice Necromancer up through Miscarcand Lich.

**Third priority**: "The Slave-Queen's Rebellion" — Umaril's two-phase lane resurrection, combined with an Ayleid-guardian deep bench, gives this a strong identity. The Welkynd-stone supports and necropolis-lane mechanics add novelty.
