# Hammerfell Adventure Research

Research for designing Path of Tamriel adventures in the Hammerfell region.

---

## Lore Overview

Hammerfell is a vast western province of Tamriel, homeland of the Redguards. Its landscape ranges from the enormous Alik'r Desert in the northwest, to coastal port cities, jungles, grasslands, and the mountainous Craglorn region in the northeast. It borders High Rock to the north, Skyrim to the northeast, and Cyrodiil to the east/southeast.

### Key Regions & Cities

| Region/City | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Alik'r Desert** | Vast desert dominating northwestern Hammerfell. Nomadic tribes, ancient Yokudan ruins, undead threats |
| **Sentinel** | Major Crown city in the north. Cultural capital, home to famous heroes and poets |
| **Stros M'Kai** | Island off the southern coast. Setting of TES Adventures: Redguard. Site of Cyrus's rebellion |
| **Rihad** | Southern coastal city, Forebear stronghold |
| **Taneth** | Southern city, fell to the Aldmeri Dominion during the Great War |
| **Dragonstar** | Northeastern city, partially controlled by Nords since 3E 396 |
| **Hegathe** | Crown capital, traditional seat of power |
| **Craglorn** | Mountainous northeastern region. Celestial threats, Dwemer ruins, Star-Gazer scholars |
| **Bangkorai** | Border region shared with High Rock. Orc strongholds, Imperial forts |
| **Hel Ra Citadel** | Ancient Yokudan training fortress in Craglorn |

### Races & Peoples

- **Redguards** — dominant race, descended from Yokudan refugees (the Ra Gada). Tamriel's most accomplished warriors
- **Orcs** — present in Bangkorai and Wrothgarian border areas (Bangkorai Butcher is in the card catalog)
- **Dwemer (extinct)** — colonized Hammerfell ~1E 420, disappeared 1E 700. Ruins throughout, especially Craglorn
- **Nords** — control part of Dragonstar
- **Imperials** — present during Empire periods
- **Thalmor/Altmer** — Fourth Era invaders during the Great War

### Key Factions

- **Crowns** — Yokudan traditionalists. Honor the old ways, Na-Totambu nobility. Capital: Hegathe/Sentinel
- **Forebears** — Pragmatic, Imperial-influenced Redguards descended from Ra Gada warrior-lords. Southern cities
- **Lhotunics** — Short-lived moderate faction, rejected by both sides
- **Restless League** — Rebel group from Stros M'Kai, led by Cyrus against Imperial occupation
- **Aldmeri Dominion/Thalmor** — Attacked Hammerfell during the Great War; Hammerfell seceded from the Empire rather than accept the White-Gold Concordat's terms

## Sword-Singers & the Ansei

The most iconic element of Redguard culture. Sword-Singers were an ancient Yokudan warrior order following "The Way of the Sword." Their blades literally sang in battle.

### The Shehai (Spirit Sword)
The pinnacle of Sword-Singer mastery — manifesting a blade of pure spirit/thought. The greatest Ansei could create brilliant ethereal blades that cut through anything.

### Ansei Ranks
- **First-Rank** — Could create a misty, pale Shehai after renouncing material possessions
- **Second-Rank** — Brilliant combat-ready Shehais; considered an unstoppable force
- Higher ranks reportedly could cut through the laws of nature themselves (the "Pankratosword" that allegedly sunk Yokuda)

### Training
- Recruits joined at age 11 (Brothers of the Blade / Maidens of the Spirit Sword)
- Advanced training required the "Walkabout" — years-long wilderness journey emulating Frandar Hunding
- Training grounds: Abbey of the Blades, Hel Ra Citadel, Rahni'Za (five increasingly difficult tests)

### Decline
After peace came, teachings faded within three generations. By the Third Era, most believed the Ansei never existed. But Redguards remain Tamriel's most capable warriors.
