# High Rock — Adventure Research

## 1. Lore Overview

High Rock is a province in northwestern Tamriel, homeland of the Bretons — a race of men descended from the intermingling of Nedic humans and Aldmeri (specifically Direnni) elves during the Merethic Era. Though their elven ancestry is largely hidden by their human appearance, Bretons inherit a natural affinity for magic unmatched among the races of men. The province is defined by feudal politics, knightly orders, arcane mastery, and a long history of internal squabbling between petty kingdoms.

### Geography

High Rock is bounded by the Sea of Ghosts to the north, Hammerfell to the south and east, Skyrim to the east, and the Abecean Sea to the west. The terrain varies widely:

- **Western coastline** — The Iliac Bay coast, home to the great trading cities of Daggerfall and Wayrest
- **Northern highlands** — Rivenspire's gloomy, jagged rock formations and bleak lowlands
- **Wrothgarian Mountains** — Eastern range bordering Skyrim, home to Orsinium (the Orc capital, repeatedly built and destroyed)
- **The Western Reach** — Rugged, wild highlands claimed by the Reachmen, extending into Skyrim
- **Bangkorai** — Southern borderlands connecting High Rock to Hammerfell, featuring highlands and desert transition
- **Glenumbra** — Heartland forests and moors surrounding Daggerfall
- **Isle of Balfiera** — Island in the Iliac Bay housing the Adamantine Tower (oldest known structure in Tamriel), seat of Clan Direnni

## 3. Races & Peoples

### Bretons
- Descendants of Nedic-Aldmeri hybrid bloodlines from the Direnni era
- Natural magical affinity; the most magically gifted race of men
- Culturally defined by feudal politics, chivalric knightly orders, and arcane scholarship
- Society organized around competing kingdoms, noble houses, and religious/knightly orders
- Druidic spellcraft is said to be the first truly Breton magical tradition

### Reachmen (Witchmen of High Rock)
- Related to Bretons but culturally distinct; inhabit the Western Reach
- Worship Hircine and revere Hagraven matriarchs
- Organized into clans led by Hagravens or Briarheart warriors
- Practice nature magic and blood rituals; Briarhearts replace their hearts with poisoned briars
- Historically despised as barbarians by both Bretons and Nords
- The Forsworn are Fourth Era Reachmen rebels in Skyrim's Reach

### Orcs (Orsimer)
- Not native to High Rock but have long contested the Wrothgarian Mountains
- Built and lost Orsinium multiple times (First, Second, Third, Fourth Eras)
- Fierce warrior culture with clan-based society
- Periodic raiding of Breton settlements and Bjoulsae River trade routes

### Clan Direnni (High Elves)
- Aristocratic Altmer merchant clan who once ruled all of High Rock
- Credited as ancestors of the Bretons through interbreeding
- Pioneers of alchemy, conjuration, and enchanting
- Now confined to the Isle of Balfiera and the Adamantine Tower

## 6. Signature Cultural Elements

- **Feudal Fragmentation** — High Rock's defining trait: a patchwork of competing petty kingdoms, noble houses, and city-states. Political intrigue, marriage alliances, and succession crises are constant.
- **Knightly Orders** — Every major kingdom maintains a chivalric order. Knights of the Dragon (Daggerfall), Knights of the Rose (Wayrest), Order of the Raven (Dwynnen). Honor, tournaments, and noble quests are central to Breton identity.
- **Innate Magical Talent** — Bretons have the highest natural magical resistance and affinity among men, inherited from Direnni blood. Ward magic and conjuration are signature Breton disciplines.
- **The Adamantine Tower** — Oldest structure in Tamriel (ME 2500), located on Balfiera. Built by the gods themselves for a Convention. Now held by the remnant of Clan Direnni.
- **Orsinium's Cycle** — The Orc capital in the Wrothgarian Mountains has been built, besieged, and destroyed at least four times across the Eras. This cycle of destruction and rebuilding is central to both Orc and Breton regional identity.
- **Reachmen Witchcraft** — The Western Reach harbors a parallel culture of Hagraven-worshipping hedge mages, Briarhearts, and nature shamans — feared as "Witchmen" by civilized Bretons.
- **The Warp in the West** — The single most important event in High Rock's history. A Dragon Break caused by the activation of the Numidium that paradoxically resolved all possible outcomes of the Daggerfall main quest simultaneously, merging dozens of petty kingdoms into a few large ones.

### Proposal 1: "The Warp in the West"

**Difficulty**: 3 (Hard)

**Setting**: Daggerfall → Shedungent (Wrothgarian Mountains) → Mannimarco's Lair → Aetherius

**Storyline**: Emperor Uriel Septim VII sends you to investigate the haunting of Daggerfall by King Lysandus's ghost. The trail leads through court intrigue, the mad witch Nulfaga's castle, and ultimately to a confrontation with Mannimarco, the King of Worms, who seeks to use the Mantella — the heart of the Numidium — to achieve godhood. Stop the necromancer before his dark ascension tears the fabric of time itself.

- **Boss Mechanic**: Summons creatures from the player's discard pile, turning fallen allies against them. Thematically represents his mastery over death itself.

- **Deck theme**: Worm Cult — Black Worm Necromancer, Black Worm Neophyte, Worm King's Agent, skeletons, spirits
- **Lore**: Leader of a Worm Nest, Mannimarco's lieutenants who recruit dark mages with promises of power

2. **Worm Cult Anchorite** (mini-boss) — Worm-themed necromancers: Black Worm Necromancer, Black Worm Neophyte, Worm Thralls, skeleton tokens, raise-dead spells. Differentiated from existing Necromancer enemy by emphasizing Worm-specific cards and spirit creatures rather than pure Draugr.

## 11. Recommendation

**Build first: "The Warp in the West"**

This is the clear winner on every axis:

1. **Card depth** — 44 Breton cards, 39 Ward cards, and deep undead/spirit enemy pools make deck construction straightforward. No thin pools to worry about.
2. **Lore iconicness** — The TES II: Daggerfall main quest is one of the franchise's most legendary storylines. Mannimarco, the Warp in the West, and the Numidium are top-tier Elder Scrolls lore.
3. **Boss quality** — Mannimarco exists as a 10c legendary with a thematically perfect ability (summoning from opponent's discard = raising the dead). No import needed.
4. **Mechanical uniqueness** — INT/END Sorcerer with Ward/Conjuration is completely distinct from all four existing adventure decks. No attribute overlap.

**Build second: "The Siege of Wrothgar"** — The Orc enemy pool (34 cards with tribal synergy) is excellent, Gortwog exists as a boss, and STR/END equipment warrior is a satisfying archetype. This pairs well with "Warp in the West" since they represent opposite ends of High Rock's identity: arcane vs martial.

**Hold for later: "Darkness Over Rivenspire"** and **"Bloodthorn Rising"** are both strong concepts but need boss card imports to execute. Rivenspire's vampire theme is compelling, but the WIL/END player deck has fewer Breton-specific cards. Bloodthorn Rising has the weakest enemy pool depth (werewolves: 11, Reachmen: 6).
