# Black Marsh Adventure Research

Research for designing Path of Tamriel adventures in the Black Marsh region.

---

## Lore Overview

Black Marsh (also called Argonia) is a dense, poisonous swampland in southeastern Tamriel, homeland of the Argonians — a reptilian humanoid race with deep spiritual ties to the Hist, a species of ancient sentient trees. The province is largely impenetrable to outsiders; toxic fumes, impassable terrain, and hostile fauna make the interior lethal to non-Argonians. Middle Argonia, the dark heart of the marsh, is so hostile that even siege weapons cannot function there.

Black Marsh is unique among Tamriel's provinces for its deeply alien culture — the Hist hive-mind, Sithis worship, the Shadowscale assassin tradition, and a society that has successfully repelled every major invasion in recent history, including charging *into* the Oblivion Gates during the Oblivion Crisis and forcing the Daedra to close their own portals.

## Signature Cultural Elements

### The Hist Connection
The most defining feature of Argonian culture. Argonians lick Hist sap on their Hatching Day, gaining their soul and a lifelong telepathic bond. The Hist function as a hive-mind, can recall Argonians from across Tamriel, and demonstrated prophetic ability by foreseeing the Oblivion Crisis. Cards: **Hist Speaker**, **Hist Grove**, **Invoker of the Hist**, **Thorn Histmage**, **Preserver of the Root**, **Tree Minder**

### Sithis Worship
Argonians revere Sithis as the original creator — not the malevolent force other cultures perceive. The Teeth of Sithis is a major temple in Murkmire. Cards: **Wrath of Sithis**, **Brotherhood Suspect**, **Stalking Shadowscale**

### Shadowscale Tradition
Argonians born under the Shadow sign become assassins. Deeply tied to the Dark Brotherhood. Cards: **Shadowscale Hunter**, **Shadowscale Partisan**, **Stalking Shadowscale**

### Magicka Ramp (Root Magic)
Argonian cards have a strong magicka-ramp sub-theme, reflecting Hist magic and connection to the Root. Cards: **Tree Minder**, **Hist Speaker**, **Thorn Histmage**, **Blackwood Alchemist**, **Blackwood Distiller**, **Hist Grove**, **Archein Venomtongue** (Slay: +1 max magicka)

## Adventure Theme Proposals

### 1. "Root and Ruin" (Hist Ramp — Murkmire Expedition)

**Setting**: Lilmoth docks -> Murkmire swamp trails -> ancient Xanmeer ruins -> Teeth of Sithis temple -> Blackrose Prison

**Storyline**: A Cyrodilic Collections expedition into Murkmire to recover the Remnant of Argon goes wrong when the Blackguards seize the relic. The player must navigate hostile swamp fauna, earn the trust of suspicious local Argonian tribes, and assault Blackrose Prison to stop the Blackguards from unleashing the relic's power.

**Deck archetype**: AGI/END Scout — magicka ramp + Lethal. The signature Argonian mechanical identity: ramp to 7+ magicka to activate conditional buffs, use Lethal creatures for efficient removal, and deploy big finishers.

**Core cards**:
- **Black Marsh Warden** (legendary anchor) — army-building payoff for ramp
- **Thorn Histmage** (dual AGI/END) — ramp + grows as magicka increases
- **Blackwood Distiller** (dual AGI/END) — Pilfer/Slay magicka engine
- **Tree Minder** — early ramp, Guard
- **Hist Speaker** — passive +1 max magicka
- **Archein Venomtongue** — Lethal + Slay ramp
- **Archein Elite** — pure Lethal body
- **Preserver of the Root** — 4/4 that becomes 6/6 Guard at 7+ magicka
- **Stormhold Henchman** — 2/2 becomes 4/4 at 7+ magicka
- **Blackwood Alchemist** — grows on magicka gain
- **Sly Marshblade** — Last Gasp draw at 7+ magicka
- **Blackrose Herbalist** — sustain via healing
- **Shadowfen Priest** — Silence/support removal
- **Swamp Leviathan** — top-end finisher (8/8 for 7)
- **Hist Grove** (support) — ultimate ramp payoff (8/8s in both lanes)

**Enemy decks**: Swamp creatures (Wamasu, Crocodiles, Giant Snakes, Lurchers), Blackguard bandits, corrupted Argonian cultists

**Boss**: Blackguard Warlord (custom enemy) — or **Red Bramman** (already a legendary card), the pirate king of Blackrose. Special mechanic: "Silences and Shackles your board on entry, then summons Blackguard reinforcements each turn."

**Node highlights**:
- Boon: "Blessing of the Hist" (Hist-magic themed)
- Event: "Drink of the Hist" — sacrifice 5 HP to gain +1 max magicka for the rest of the run
- Event: "Tribal Suspicion" — an Argonian tribe tests you. Pass: gain a recruit. Fail: fight a mini-boss
- Shop: "Lilmoth Dockside Market"
- Creature augment: "Hist Sap Infusion"
- Mini-boss: Blackguard Captain or a Wamasu Alpha

**Feasibility**: Card pool depth: **Excellent** (15+ core Argonian cards + strong ramp/lethal support). Boss: **Ready** (Red Bramman is a perfect legendary boss). Lore: **Deep** (Murkmire DLC + Blackrose Prison).

### 2. "The Floating City" (Umbriel Crisis — Lilmoth / Middle Argonia)

**Setting**: Lilmoth streets -> the corrupted Hist tree -> swamp trek through Middle Argonia -> approach to Umbriel -> confrontation inside the floating city

**Storyline**: The rogue Hist of Lilmoth has summoned the floating city Umbriel, which raises the dead and mind-controls Argonians. The player must break free of its influence, navigate the deadly interior of Middle Argonia (where even the Hist have gone silent), and infiltrate Umbriel itself to destroy the Ingenium soul engine and stop Vuhon.

**Deck archetype**: AGI/END Scout — Veteran + Last Gasp. Thematically, the player's creatures must "prove themselves" (Veteran) against Umbriel's undead horde. Last Gasp effects represent the cycle of death and rebirth central to the Hist/Sithis philosophy.

**Core cards**:
- **Steel-Eyed Visionary** (legendary anchor) — Veteran payoff that transforms your board
- **Invoker of the Hist** (legendary) — Veteran magicka restore, Hist connection
- **Black Marsh Centurion** — Veteran: Guard, massive body
- **Black Marsh Prodigy** — Veteran: +4/+4, high growth potential
- **Great Moot Squire** — cheap Veteran creature
- **Shadowscale Hunter** — Veteran: Move (evasion)
- **Sly Marshblade** — Last Gasp: draw
- **Fighters Guild Recruit** — Prophecy, Guard, Lethal
- **Twin Lamps Consul** — Plot: Lethal + Guard
- **Red Bramman** — finisher (Silence + Shackle lane)
- **Sails-Through-Storms** — Pilfer summon, alternate win condition
- **Stalking Shadowscale** — Slay: grows
- **Shadowfen Priest** — tech card

**Enemy decks**: Umbriel zombies (undead creatures), mind-controlled Argonians, An-Xileel traitors, Daedric entities

**Boss**: Vuhon (custom) — High Lord of Umbriel. Special mechanic: "At the start of each turn, summon a 2/2 Undead from both players' discard piles" (reflecting Umbriel's soul-harvesting). Or the Ingenium itself as an invulnerable support the player must destroy through a special condition.

**Node highlights**:
- Boon: "Hist Sap Vision" (foresight-themed)
- Event: "The Rogue Hist" — drink corrupted sap for power at a cost, or resist
- Event: "Mere-Glim's Warning" — choose to trust the Argonian or go alone
- Shop: "Scavenger's Salvage" (looting Umbriel's fallen)
- Action augment: "Oblivion Tincture"
- Mini-boss: An-Xileel Betrayer (mind-controlled Argonian commander)

**Feasibility**: Card pool depth: **Good** (Veteran Argonians are 6+ cards, supported by generic AGI/END). Boss: **Custom only** (Vuhon doesn't exist as a card). Lore: **Deep** (two TES novels' worth of material).

### 3. "The Shadowscale's Last Hunt" (Assassin Theme — Stormhold / Deep Marsh)

**Setting**: Stormhold -> Dark Brotherhood sanctuary -> Shadowscale training grounds -> deep swamp pursuit -> target's stronghold

**Storyline**: The player takes on the role of one of the last Shadowscales, carrying out a final contract that will determine the fate of the order. The target: a rogue An-Xileel leader who has been corrupting the Hist trees for personal power. Each node represents a step in the assassination — gathering intel, eliminating guards, and finally striking at the target.

**Deck archetype**: AGI-heavy (splash END) — Lethal/Slay assassin deck. Kill efficiently, grow stronger with each kill, use stealth and movement to control the board.

**Core cards**:
- **Sails-Through-Storms** (legendary anchor) — Pilfer summons creatures, snowball potential
- **Swims-at-Night** (legendary) — trickster, generates 0-cost tools
- **Shadowscale Partisan** — Lethal + Lethal synergy
- **Shadowscale Hunter** — Veteran: Move
- **Stalking Shadowscale** — Slay: +1/+1
- **Archein Venomtongue** — Lethal + Slay ramp
- **Archein Elite** — pure Lethal
- **Fighters Guild Recruit** — Prophecy, Guard, Lethal
- **Twin Lamps Consul** — Plot: Lethal + Guard
- **Territorial Viper** — Charge + Lethal, the ultimate assassin card
- **An-Xileel Invader** — 5/3 aggressive body
- **Brotherhood Suspect** — flavorful Last Gasp
- **Helstrom Footpad** — cheap Argonian body
- **Soulrest Marshal** — cost reduction payoff
- **Thieves Guild Recruit** — draw + cost reduction

**Enemy decks**: An-Xileel loyalists, corrupted Hist creatures, swamp monsters guarding the path

**Boss**: Rogue An-Xileel Warlord (custom) — or reskin **Red Bramman** as a corrupted leader. Special mechanic: "Starts with two 1/1 Guards and summons a new one each turn. When a Guard dies, all enemy creatures gain +1/+0" (representing growing alarm).

**Node highlights**:
- Boon: "Shadow's Blessing" (assassin-themed — first creature you summon each match gains Cover)
- Event: "The Contract" — choose your approach: stealth (sacrifice HP, skip a combat) or direct assault (fight, gain a reward)
- Event: "Veezara's Memory" — the last Shadowscale shares wisdom (buff a creature permanently)
- Shop: "Black Market Contact"
- Creature augment: "Poisoned Blade" (grant Lethal to a creature)
- Mini-boss: An-Xileel Enforcer

**Feasibility**: Card pool depth: **Good** (Lethal/Slay cards are plentiful in AGI/END, plus assassin Agility staples). Boss: **Custom** (no named boss card, but Red Bramman can anchor). Lore: **Solid** (Shadowscale tradition is well-documented but not deeply storied like the Oblivion Crisis).

### 4. "Gates of the Marsh" (Oblivion Crisis — An-Xileel Counterattack)

**Setting**: Stormhold -> Oblivion Gate site -> fighting through the Deadlands -> sealing gates across the marsh -> final push into Oblivion

**Storyline**: The Oblivion Crisis strikes Black Marsh, but unlike the rest of Tamriel, the Argonians don't just defend — they attack. Called back by the Hist, the An-Xileel rally Argonian warriors to charge through the Oblivion Gates, fighting with such ferocity that Mehrunes Dagon's own Dremora are forced to close their portals to stop being overrun. The player leads this legendary counterattack.

**Deck archetype**: AGI/END Scout — aggressive Veteran/ramp hybrid. The deck wants to attack relentlessly (triggering Veteran) while ramping to deploy overwhelming force.

**Core cards**:
- **Invoker of the Hist** (legendary anchor) — Veteran restores magicka, enabling massive turns
- **Steel-Eyed Visionary** (legendary) — Veteran transforms board's power
- **Black Marsh Centurion** — Veteran: Guard, anchors defense
- **Black Marsh Prodigy** — Veteran: explosive growth
- **An-Xileel Invader** — 5/3 aggressive, faction-named
- **Great Moot Squire** — cheap Veteran starter
- **Shadowscale Hunter** — Veteran: Move, evasive attacker
- **Tree Minder** — ramp + Guard
- **Thorn Histmage** — ramp + grows
- **Archein Venomtongue** — Lethal Slay ramp
- **Swamp Leviathan** — top-end finisher
- **Fighters Guild Recruit** — defensive Prophecy
- **Hist Grove** — ultimate payoff (8/8s in both lanes)

**Enemy decks**: Daedra (Flame Atronachs, Dremora, Scamps), Oblivion Gate guardians

**Boss**: Dremora General (custom) — or **Mehrunes Dagon's Champion**. Special mechanic: "Starts with an Oblivion Gate support that summons a 3/3 Dremora each turn. Destroy the Gate to stop reinforcements." Could also use existing Invade/Oblivion Gate mechanics if engine supports it.

**Node highlights**:
- Boon: "Call of the Hist" (the Hist empower you)
- Event: "Through the Gate" — charge into Oblivion (gain a powerful creature but take 5 damage) or hold the line
- Event: "The Hist's Command" — the trees offer a vision of the future (discover a boon, but lose a card from deck)
- Shop: "Wartime Provisions"
- Creature augment: "Hist-Touched" (Hist magic empowerment)
- Mini-boss: Dremora Kynval

**Feasibility**: Card pool depth: **Good** (Veteran Argonians + ramp core, ~12 Argonians + END/AGI support). Boss: **Custom only** (no Daedric boss card specifically for this). Lore: **Deep** (the Argonian counter-invasion of Oblivion is one of the most legendary events in TES lore).

## Recommendation

**Build "Root and Ruin" first.** It has the strongest combination of:

1. **Card pool depth** — the magicka ramp Argonian package is the deepest and most mechanically cohesive cluster (15+ cards with direct synergy). Cards like Thorn Histmage, Blackwood Alchemist, and Stormhold Henchman all activate at 7+ magicka, creating clear gameplay goals.

2. **Boss quality** — Red Bramman already exists as a 9-cost legendary card with a devastating Silence+Shackle effect, making for a memorable and mechanically distinct boss fight. No custom boss needed.

3. **Lore iconicness** — Murkmire is the most directly "Black Marsh" setting available, featuring the Hist, ancient Xanmeer ruins, and the Blackguards. Players who've done ESO's Murkmire DLC will recognize it immediately.

4. **Mechanical uniqueness** — No existing adventure uses a magicka ramp deck. The "ramp to 7+ to unlock conditional power" identity plays fundamentally differently from the STR/INT item decks, AGI slay decks, and WIL token decks in other regions. This gives Black Marsh its own feel.

**Second priority**: "The Floating City" — the Umbriel storyline is fantastic and wholly unique, but it requires a custom boss (Vuhon). Consider it once card imports make a Vuhon card available.
