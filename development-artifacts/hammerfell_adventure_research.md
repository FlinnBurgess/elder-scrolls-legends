# Hammerfell Adventure Research

Research for designing Path of Tamriel adventures in the Hammerfell region.

---

## Lore Overview

Hammerfell is a vast western province of Tamriel, homeland of the Redguards. Its landscape ranges from the enormous Alik'r Desert in the northwest, to coastal port cities, jungles, grasslands, and the mountainous Craglorn region in the northeast. It borders High Rock to the north, Skyrim to the northeast, and Cyrodiil to the east/southeast.

### Key Regions & Cities

| Region/City | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Alik'r Desert** | Vast desert dominating northwestern Hammerfell. Nomadic tribes, ancient Yokudan ruins, undead threats |
| **Sentinel** | Major Crown city in the north. Cultural capital, home to famous heroes and poets |
| **Stros M'Kai** | Island off the southern coast. Setting of TES Adventures: Redguard. Site of Cyrus's rebellion |
| **Rihad** | Southern coastal city, Forebear stronghold |
| **Taneth** | Southern city, fell to the Aldmeri Dominion during the Great War |
| **Dragonstar** | Northeastern city, partially controlled by Nords since 3E 396 |
| **Hegathe** | Crown capital, traditional seat of power |
| **Craglorn** | Mountainous northeastern region. Celestial threats, Dwemer ruins, Star-Gazer scholars |
| **Bangkorai** | Border region shared with High Rock. Orc strongholds, Imperial forts |
| **Hel Ra Citadel** | Ancient Yokudan training fortress in Craglorn |

### Races & Peoples

- **Redguards** — dominant race, descended from Yokudan refugees (the Ra Gada). Tamriel's most accomplished warriors
- **Orcs** — present in Bangkorai and Wrothgarian border areas (Bangkorai Butcher is in the card catalog)
- **Dwemer (extinct)** — colonized Hammerfell ~1E 420, disappeared 1E 700. Ruins throughout, especially Craglorn
- **Nords** — control part of Dragonstar
- **Imperials** — present during Empire periods
- **Thalmor/Altmer** — Fourth Era invaders during the Great War

### Key Factions

- **Crowns** — Yokudan traditionalists. Honor the old ways, Na-Totambu nobility. Capital: Hegathe/Sentinel
- **Forebears** — Pragmatic, Imperial-influenced Redguards descended from Ra Gada warrior-lords. Southern cities
- **Lhotunics** — Short-lived moderate faction, rejected by both sides
- **Restless League** — Rebel group from Stros M'Kai, led by Cyrus against Imperial occupation
- **Aldmeri Dominion/Thalmor** — Attacked Hammerfell during the Great War; Hammerfell seceded from the Empire rather than accept the White-Gold Concordat's terms

## Sword-Singers & the Ansei

The most iconic element of Redguard culture. Sword-Singers were an ancient Yokudan warrior order following "The Way of the Sword." Their blades literally sang in battle.

### The Shehai (Spirit Sword)
The pinnacle of Sword-Singer mastery — manifesting a blade of pure spirit/thought. The greatest Ansei could create brilliant ethereal blades that cut through anything.

### Ansei Ranks
- **First-Rank** — Could create a misty, pale Shehai after renouncing material possessions
- **Second-Rank** — Brilliant combat-ready Shehais; considered an unstoppable force
- Higher ranks reportedly could cut through the laws of nature themselves (the "Pankratosword" that allegedly sunk Yokuda)

### Training
- Recruits joined at age 11 (Brothers of the Blade / Maidens of the Spirit Sword)
- Advanced training required the "Walkabout" — years-long wilderness journey emulating Frandar Hunding
- Training grounds: Abbey of the Blades, Hel Ra Citadel, Rahni'Za (five increasingly difficult tests)

### Decline
After peace came, teachings faded within three generations. By the Third Era, most believed the Ansei never existed. But Redguards remain Tamriel's most capable warriors.

## Adventure Theme Proposals

### 1. "Swords of the Ansei" (Sword-Singer themed — Alik'r Desert / Hel Ra Citadel)

**Setting**: Deep Alik'r Desert → ancient Yokudan ruins → Hel Ra Citadel training grounds

**Storyline**: The player discovers remnants of the ancient Sword-Singer training grounds. Ghost Ansei test the worthy through increasingly difficult trials (echoing Rahni'Za's five tests). The final challenge: facing the spirit of Frandar Hunding himself, who tests whether any modern warrior can still walk the Way of the Sword.

**Deck archetype**: STR/INT Battlemage — item-heavy "Blades of Hammerfell" deck. Core cards: Merric-at-Aswala, Rihad Battlemage, Rihad Horseman, Dragonstar Rider, Alik'r Survivalist, Sentinel Battlemace, Master of Arms, Skilled Blacksmith, Crown Quartermaster, Yokudan Nightblade, Whirling Duelist, Gardener of Swords

**Enemy decks**: Undead Yokudan warriors, Dwemer automatons in the ruins, spectral Ansei trials

**Boss**: Spirit of Frandar Hunding (or Derik Hallin, the Last Ansei)

**Node highlights**:
- Boon: "Blessing of the Shehai" (item-themed boon)
- Event: "The Walkabout" — sacrifice HP to gain a powerful item
- Event: "The Book of Circles" — choose a teaching that buffs your creatures
- Shop: "Yokudan Arms Dealer"
- Mini-boss: Divad Hunding or a corrupted Ansei ghost

---

### 2. "The Siege of Stros M'Kai" (Cyrus's Rebellion — Stros M'Kai island)

**Setting**: Port town of Stros M'Kai → Catacombs → N'Gasta's island → the Restless League hideout → Governor's Palace

**Storyline**: The player arrives on Stros M'Kai during the Imperial occupation. They must rally the Restless League, deal with the necromancer N'Gasta, and ultimately confront Provisional Governor Amiel Richton to liberate the island. Based on TES Adventures: Redguard.

**Deck archetype**: AGI or AGI/STR rogue/pirate deck. Core cards: Gentleman Jim Stacey (thief flavor), plus pirate/rogue-themed Agility cards, items, and movement tricks

**Enemy decks**: Imperial soldiers, N'Gasta's undead horrors, Richton's elite guard

**Boss**: Amiel Richton (Imperial governor) — could have a special mechanic like "Imperial Reinforcements" that summons guards each turn

**Node highlights**:
- Event: "The Restless League" — choose to recruit smugglers or go alone
- Mini-boss: N'Gasta the Sload Necromancer (summons undead)
- Event: "Dwarven Ruins Beneath the City" — risk/reward treasure hunt
- Shop: "Stros M'Kai Docks" — pirate market
- Boon: "Cyrus's Resolve"

---

### 3. "Crowns and Forebears" (Civil War — Sentinel / Hegathe / Rihad)

**Setting**: Begins in Sentinel → travels through contested territory → Hegathe → climactic battle

**Storyline**: The ancient schism between Crowns and Forebears has erupted again. The player must navigate the civil war, choosing sides at branching points. Crown path leads through Hegathe's traditionalist trials; Forebear path goes through Rihad's political machinations. Both converge on a final threat — the Thalmor exploiting the division to invade.

**Deck archetype**: STR/INT Battlemage — rally and item hybrid. Core cards: Skar Drillmaster, Bal Isra Warrider, Seasoned Captain, Rihad Battlemage, Crown Quartermaster, Withered Hand Cultist

**Enemy decks**: Opposing faction warriors (Crown or Forebear depending on path), Thalmor agents, Aldmeri Dominion soldiers

**Boss**: A Thalmor General (Altmer) — or High King Thassad II's vengeful ghost if going darker

**Node highlights**:
- Branching choice: "Crown Path" vs "Forebear Path" with different encounters and reinforcements
- Event: "The Lhotunics' Plea" — a moderate faction asks for help
- Mini-boss: Rival faction champion
- Boon: "Crown's Tradition" or "Forebear's Pragmatism" (different buffs based on path)
- Shop: "Sentinel Bazaar" or "Rihad War Market"

---

### 4. "Stars Over Craglorn" (Celestial Threat — Craglorn)

**Setting**: Star-Gazer observatory → Dwemer ruins → Hel Ra Citadel → Celestial confrontation

**Storyline**: The constellations over Craglorn have gone dark. The Celestial Warrior, once a guardian of Hammerfell, has been corrupted and now terrorizes the region with an army of atronachs and fallen warriors. The player must gather Star-Gazer knowledge, delve into Dwemer ruins for ancient weapons, and confront the Celestial threat. (Based on ESO: Craglorn content.)

**Deck archetype**: INT-heavy or STR/INT — support and magic synergy. Core cards: Craglorn Scavenger, Conjuration Scholar, Keeper of Whispers, Dragonstar Rider, plus atronach and support cards

**Enemy decks**: Corrupted celestial warriors, Dwemer automatons (Spider Workers, Centurions), atronachs

**Boss**: The Celestial Warrior — could have a unique mechanic like "Constellation Power" that buffs all enemies at the start of each turn

**Node highlights**:
- Event: "Star-Gazer's Vision" — choose a constellation blessing
- Creature augment: "Dwemer Forge" — enhance creatures with Dwemer tech
- Mini-boss: Dwemer Centurion
- Event: "The Fallen Mage" — corrupted Star-Gazer offers dark power
- Boon: "The Serpent's Cunning" or "The Warrior's Strength"

## Summary: Recommended Priority

| # | Adventure | Deck Theme | Boss | Card Catalog Fit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Swords of the Ansei** | STR/INT items (Battlemage) | Frandar Hunding | Excellent — deep item synergy card pool with many Hammerfell-named cards |
| 2 | **Siege of Stros M'Kai** | AGI rogue/pirate | Amiel Richton | Good — Gentleman Jim Stacey fits perfectly, "Siege of Stros M'Kai" card exists |
| 3 | **Crowns and Forebears** | STR/INT rally + items | Thalmor General | Good — rally cards + Crown Quartermaster, branching path is unique |
| 4 | **Stars Over Craglorn** | INT support/magic | Celestial Warrior | Moderate — Craglorn Scavenger fits, would need more Dwemer/celestial enemy cards |

**Strongest recommendation**: "Swords of the Ansei" — the item-synergy Redguard card pool is the deepest and most thematically cohesive. It leverages the most iconic Hammerfell lore (Sword-Singers), has a clear boss (Frandar Hunding), and the STR/INT Battlemage item archetype is well-represented in the existing catalog.
