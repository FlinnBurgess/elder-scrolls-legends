# Valenwood Adventure Research

## 1. Lore Overview

Valenwood is the densely forested province in southwestern Tamriel, homeland of the Bosmer (Wood Elves). Dominated by massive graht-oak trees that tower high enough to blot out the sun, Valenwood's cities are built within the boughs of these living giants. The province is defined by the **Green Pact** — a sacred covenant with the forest-god Y'ffre in which the Bosmer pledge never to harm the forests in exchange for Y'ffre's protection. This means the Bosmer are strict carnivores who use only bone, animal, and insect products, importing any wood they need.

Valenwood's history is shaped by the **Camoran Dynasty**, which has ruled since the First Era, and by repeated foreign interference — from the Alessian Empire, to Elsweyr border wars, to Thalmor annexation. The province's most terrifying weapon is the **Wild Hunt**, a forbidden ritual in which Bosmer shed their Y'ffre-given forms and transform into monstrous, primordial creatures.

## 3. Races & Peoples

- **Bosmer (Wood Elves)** — Dominant race. Agile, skilled archers and scouts. Bound by the Green Pact. Organized in tribal clans under the Camoran Dynasty. Consume only meat; drink jagga (fermented pig's milk) and rotmeth (fermented meat beverage)
- **Altmer (High Elves)** — Present as Aldmeri Dominion administrators and Thalmor agents. Built cities like Marbruk. Relationship ranges from alliance to occupation
- **Khajiit** — Eastern neighbors in Reaper's March. Border conflicts (Five Year War) but also Dominion allies
- **Imperials** — Historical invaders (Alessian Empire, Colovian occupation of Arenthia)

## 6. Signature Cultural Elements

- **The Green Pact** — Sacred oath to never harm Valenwood's plant life. Enforced by the Spinners of Y'ffre. Bosmer eat only meat as a result
- **The Wild Hunt** — Terrifying ritual where Bosmer shed their forms and become monstrous, shape-shifting creatures. Irreversible. Used as ultimate weapon of last resort
- **The Silvenar & Green Lady** — Spiritual/physical duality. The Silvenar embodies Bosmer diplomacy and spirituality; the Green Lady embodies their ferocity and connection to nature. They must bond through marriage
- **Rotmeth** — Fermented meat beverage, sacred ritual drink. No Bosmer wedding can occur without it
- **The Mourning War** — Tribal tradition of replacing fallen members via hostage-taking raids on neighboring tribes
- **Graht-oak cities** — Bosmer cities built in living trees. Elden Root is built on the largest. Falinesti is a tree that walks between seasonal sites
- **Meat Mandate** — When enemies are defeated, the Green Pact requires their flesh be consumed, not left to rot

### Proposal 1: Paradise Lost

**Setting:** From the forests of Grahtwood through Oblivion Gates into Mankar Camoran's Paradise (Gaiar Alata)

**Storyline:** The Mythic Dawn cult has opened Oblivion Gates across Valenwood, pouring Daedra into the ancient forests. As the Green Pact itself is threatened by the fires of Oblivion, a hero must fight through cultists and Dremora, enter Camoran's twisted Paradise, and slay the cult's leader before Mehrunes Dagon's invasion consumes all of Valenwood.

**Difficulty:** 3

#### Deck Design

- **Archetype:** STR/INT Battlemage — action synergy with aggressive creatures
- **Mechanical identity:** Play damage actions to trigger Crystal Tower Crafter and Telvanni Arcanist value engines while Mankar Camoran converts each action into an Invade trigger. STR creatures provide board presence with Prophecy and damage-on-summon effects.

**Core Cards (15):**

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mankar Camoran | Legendary | STR/INT | 4 | 4/4 | **Deck anchor** — Invade on every action, Gates get +4/+0 |
| Syl, Duchess of Dementia | Legendary | STR | 6 | 7/6 | Big body, lane manipulation — Wood Elf legendary |
| Green Pact Ambusher | Epic | STR | 2 | 4/1 | Prophecy Guard — cheap defensive threat |
| Grahtwood Ambusher | Epic | STR | 5 | 4/2 | Prophecy — deal 1 to all lane enemies |
| Earthbone Spinner | Epic | STR | 4 | 3/2 | Silence utility vs buffed enemies |
| Valenwood Huntsman | Common | STR | 3 | 3/1 | Summon: deal 1 damage — Valenwood flavor |
| Sharpshooter Scout | Common | STR | 1 | 1/1 | Prophecy — cheap removal trigger |
| Silvenar Tracker | Rare | STR | 2 | 3/1 | Conditional Charge — aggressive 2-drop |
| Crystal Tower Crafter | Common | INT | 1 | 1/1 | **Action synergy engine** — grows with each action |
| Telvanni Arcanist | Rare | INT | 3 | 3/3 | Draw random action — card advantage |
| Lightning Bolt | Common | INT | 4 | — | Hard removal — 4 damage |
| Firebolt | Common | INT | 1 | — | Cheap removal — 2 damage |

**Legendary anchor:** Mankar Camoran — his ability is the thematic and mechanical centerpiece. Every action played Invades, creating Oblivion Gates that grow with +4/+0. This creates a unique "action-battlemage" playstyle where spells fuel both removal AND a growing board threat.

**Mechanical coherence:** The deck plays action-heavy to trigger Crystal Tower Crafter (grows per action), Telvanni Arcanist (draws actions), and Mankar Camoran (Invades per action). STR creatures provide early board presence and Prophecy defense. The result is a tempo deck that transitions into an overwhelming late game through Gate tokens.

#### Boss & Enemies

- **Final boss: Mankar Camoran** — fought in his Paradise (Gaiar Alata). Special mechanic: starts with an Oblivion Gate support in play that summons a Daedra each turn. The player must burn through his forces while dealing with escalating Daedra pressure.
- **Mini-boss: Raven Camoran** — Mankar's son, a Mythic Dawn lieutenant. Encountered guarding the Oblivion Gate portal.
- **Enemy deck themes:**
  - *Mythic Dawn Cultists* (Invade cards + INT mage creatures) — for early/mid encounters
  - *Oblivion Daedra* (Daedra subtype cards: Dremora, Atronachs, Scamps) — for later encounters
  - *Paradise Dremora* (high-power Daedra: Dremora Markynaz, Spiteful Dremora) — for boss fight
- **Enemy races:** Daedra, High Elf (cultists), Dremora

#### Adventure Nodes

- **Boons:** "Blessing of Auri-El" (Aldmeri divine protection), "Y'ffre's Resolve" (Green Pact strength)
- **Events:**
  - *"The Informant"* — A Mythic Dawn deserter offers information about the cult's plans. Trust them (gain intel: peek at enemy deck) or arrest them (gain a guard recruit).
  - *"The Burning Grove"* — An Oblivion Gate has opened in a sacred grove. Close it quickly (-5 Max HP, remove an enemy from next fight) or detour around (no cost, no benefit).
- **Shop:** "Elden Root Armory" — arms dealer in the Dominion capital
- **Reinforcement:** "Dominion Volunteers" — Bosmer and Altmer rallying to fight
- **Augment nodes:** "Sigil Stone Forge" (creature augment — harness captured Daedric energy), "Scrolls of Xarxes" (action augment — forbidden knowledge)

### Proposal 2: Heart of the Wild

**Setting:** The deep forests of Malabal Tor, from the outskirts to the holy city of Silvenar and the sacred Treehenge

**Storyline:** Ulthorn the Hound, a Bosmer corrupted by the Daedric Prince Hircine, has seized the city of Silvenar and captured the Spinners who maintain the Green Pact. With the Silvenar imprisoned and the Green Lady forced into hiding, Hircine's influence spreads like rot through the forest. Rally the Bosmer clans, free the Spinners, and confront the Hound before Valenwood's spiritual heart is consumed by the Hunting Grounds.

**Difficulty:** 2

#### Deck Design

- **Archetype:** AGI/WIL Scout — Bosmer scouts and nature spirits with divine support
- **Mechanical identity:** Generate value through Pilfer triggers and Animal summons. Spriggan/Animal tribal synergy with Shadowgreen Elder as the late-game payoff. WIL provides defensive guards and removal to protect the aggressive AGI creatures.

### Proposal 3: Roots of Corruption

**Setting:** The misty forests of Greenshade, from the port city of Woodhearth through the ancient ruins of Hectahame to the Heart of Valenwood

**Storyline:** Prince Naemon of the Altmer, slain during the Alliance War, has returned as a vengeful shade. His spectral corruption spreads through Greenshade, twisting Spriggans into blighted horrors and turning the Wilderking's domain against itself. With the Veiled Heritance — Altmer supremacists who reject the Dominion — feeding Naemon's shade with dark magic, the Heart of Valenwood itself is at risk. Journey through the corrupted forest, cleanse the blighted growth, and destroy Naemon's shade before the corruption becomes permanent.

**Difficulty:** 2

#### Deck Design

- **Archetype:** INT/AGI Assassin — nature magic with Spriggan tribal and stealth
- **Mechanical identity:** Spriggan tribal synergy (Shadowgreen Elder buffs, Gnarl Rootbender draws from actions) combined with INT action-based removal. The deck controls the board with Lethal creatures and targeted damage while building a Spriggan/Animal army.

| Shadowgreen Elder | Epic | AGI | 7 | 5/5 | **Tribal payoff** — +2/+2 Spriggans and Animals |
| Green-Touched Spriggan | Epic | AGI | 5 | 1/5 | Gains power from healing — unique scaling |
| Wild Beastcaller | Epic | AGI | 6 | 3/4 | Summon random Animal |
| Leaflurker | Epic | AGI | 5 | 4/3 | Destroy Wounded — synergy with damage spells |

**Legendary anchor:** Cyriel (Pilfer) with Ungolim the Listener for early-game value. The dual legendary package provides aggressive early presence while the Spriggan tribal builds to a Shadowgreen Elder finisher.

**Mechanical coherence:** INT removal spells wound enemies for Leaflurker kills. The Lethal package (Nimble Ally, Imbued Bosmer) provides efficient creature removal, while the Spriggan/Animal package (Shadowgreen Elder, Wild Beastcaller, Eldergleam Matron) creates an ever-growing board.

- **Final boss: Shade of Prince Naemon** — Spectral Altmer channeling corruption into the Heart of Valenwood. Special mechanic: starts with "Corruption" support that converts one friendly Spriggan/Animal into a blighted version (enemy-controlled copy with +2/+2) each turn. The player must kill him before losing their own board to corruption.
- **Mini-boss: Veiled Heritance Warmaster** — Altmer supremacist commander channeling dark magic to fuel Naemon's shade.
- **Enemy deck themes:**
  - *Veiled Heritance Agents* (High Elf INT/WIL cards: Thalmor, mages, agents) — early/mid encounters
  - *Blighted Forest* (corrupted Spriggans + Spirits: Spirit subtype, nature-gone-wrong) — mid encounters
  - *Naemon's Court* (High Elf legendaries + Spirit cards: Ancano-style mage deck) — boss fight
- **Enemy races:** High Elf, Spirit, Spriggan (corrupted)

#### Adventure Nodes

- **Boons:** "Wilderking's Blessing" (nature spirit protection), "Spinner's Ward" (Green Pact magic)
- **Events:**

## 11. Recommendation

**Build first: Paradise Lost (STR/INT)**

This is the clear frontrunner across all criteria:

1. **Card catalog depth:** STR has 8+ thematic Wood Elf cards, INT has a deep action suite, and the 13 Invade cards + 57 Daedra provide excellent enemy deck material. No imports needed.

2. **Lore iconicness:** The Oblivion Crisis (TES IV) is arguably the most recognized Elder Scrolls storyline after Skyrim's main quest. Mankar Camoran, Mehrunes Dagon, the Mythic Dawn cult, and Oblivion Gates are instantly recognizable to any Elder Scrolls fan.

3. **Boss quality:** Mankar Camoran already exists as a legendary card with an ability (Invade + Gates +4/+0) that perfectly captures his lore identity. No custom boss creation needed — he's ready to go.

4. **Mechanical uniqueness:** An Invade/action-synergy battlemage plays completely differently from every existing adventure deck. The Companions (STR/AGI) is aggro-combat, Dark Brotherhood (AGI/END) is lethal/slay, College (INT/WIL) is control-magic, Dragons (STR/WIL) is big-creature ramp. A STR/INT action-battlemage with Invade is an entirely new archetype.

**Build second: Heart of the Wild (AGI/WIL)**

The deepest Bosmer card pool (18 AGI Wood Elves) with a unique Pilfer/Spriggan tribal identity. Requires a custom boss (The Hound) but the ESO Malabal Tor storyline provides rich material. This is the most "quintessentially Valenwood" adventure of the four proposals.
