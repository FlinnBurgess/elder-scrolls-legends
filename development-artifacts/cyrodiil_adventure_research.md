# Cyrodiil Adventure Research

Research for designing Path of Tamriel adventures in the Cyrodiil region.

---

## Lore Overview

Cyrodiil is the central province of Tamriel, known as "the Heartland." It is the seat of the Empire, home to the Imperials, and the setting of TES IV: Oblivion. The province ranges from the fertile Nibenay Valley in the east to the rugged Colovian Highlands in the west, with the Imperial City — built atop the ancient Ayleid White-Gold Tower — at its center. Cyrodiil has been the epicenter of Tamriel's most consequential events: the Alessian Slave Rebellion, the rise and fall of multiple empires, the Oblivion Crisis, and the Great War against the Aldmeri Dominion.

## Races & Peoples

- **Imperials** — dominant race. Skilled diplomats, administrators, and soldiers. Known for discipline and heavy infantry tactics
- **Ayleids (extinct)** — ancient "Wild Elves" who built the White-Gold Tower and enslaved humans. Practiced star-worship and dark magic. Ruins throughout Cyrodiil
- **Bretons/Nords/other races** — significant minorities in border cities (Bruma has Nord population, etc.)
- **Daedra** — major enemies during the Oblivion Crisis, and during ESO Blackwood (Mehrunes Dagon plots)

### Notable Creatures (Enemy Deck Material)
- **Daedra** — Dremora, Scamps, Xivilai, Daedroths, Atronachs (huge card pool: 74 cards with "Daedra" subtype)
- **Undead** — Zombies, Skeletons, Liches, Ghosts (Ayleid ruin inhabitants)
- **Minotaurs** — native to Cyrodiil's forests
- **Goblins** — common enemies in wilderness and ruins

## Signature Cultural Elements

### The Imperial Legion
Cyrodiil's defining military identity. Heavy infantry, disciplined ranks, token generation (Imperial Grunts, Colovian Troopers). The WIL attribute is packed with Imperial Legion cards that summon tokens and buff each other.

### The Arena
The Imperial City's Arena is an iconic location. Gladiatorial combat, rising through ranks. Cards: Gladiator Arena (support), Lord of the Arena.

### The Oblivion Crisis
TES IV's main storyline. Mehrunes Dagon's invasion via Oblivion Gates, the Mythic Dawn cult, and Martin Septim's sacrifice. Strong card support through the Jaws of Oblivion set: Invade mechanic, Oblivion Gates, Daedra cards, Mankar Camoran.

### Ayleid Heritage
Ancient ruins scattered across Cyrodiil. Star-worship, Welkynd/Varla stones, enslaved humanity. Umaril the Unfeathered represents this legacy.

### Guild Culture
Every major guild is headquartered in Cyrodiil — Fighters Guild, Mages Guild, Thieves Guild, Dark Brotherhood. Rich storyline material.

## Adventure Theme Proposals

### 1. "The Gates of Oblivion" (Oblivion Crisis — Imperial City / Kvatch / Bruma)

**Setting**: Kvatch ruins -> the Oblivion Gate -> Chorrol countryside -> Battle of Bruma -> Imperial City -> Mankar Camoran's Paradise

**Storyline**: The Mythic Dawn has assassinated the Emperor and opened Oblivion Gates across Cyrodiil. The player must close gates, rally the Legion, and ultimately confront Mankar Camoran in his Paradise realm to recover the Amulet of Kings before Mehrunes Dagon can manifest.

**Deck archetype**: WIL/END Spellsword — Imperial Legion token army. Summon waves of soldiers, buff them with Praefects and Trainers, and overwhelm the Daedric horde through sheer numbers and Guard walls.

**Core cards**:
- **General Tullius** (legendary anchor) — summons troops + grows as they die fighting Daedra
- **Legate Rikke** (legendary) — generates Grunts on every Imperial summon
- **Fifth Legion Trainer** — gives each summoned creature +1/+0
- **Imperial Reinforcements** — fills a lane with Grunts
- **Scouting Patrol** — Grunt in each lane for 1 cost
- **Legion Praefect** — board-wide +1/+1
- **Kvatch Soldier** — Guard, thematic (Kvatch is first to fall)
- **Praetorian Commander** — buffs entire deck +1/+1
- **Imperial Camp** — support that generates Grunts and buffs Guards
- **Watch Commander** — gives Guards +1/+2
- **Snowhawk Detachment** — Guard that grows from other Guards
- **Bruma Armorer** — gives +0/+2 to each summoned creature
- **Midnight Sweep** — Prophecy, summons Colovian Troopers in both lanes
- **Hero of Anvil** — cheap finisher when you're summoning many creatures per turn
- **Imperial Siege Engine** — grows with board size

**Enemy decks**: Mythic Dawn cultists (INT/STR mages), Daedra hordes (Dremora, Scamps, Atronachs), Undead from Oblivion

**Boss**: **Mankar Camoran** (already a legendary card, STR/INT 4/4). Special mechanic: "After every action, Invade. Oblivion Gates have +4/+0." The boss opens gates that spawn Daedra; the player must overwhelm them with the Legion.

**Node highlights**:
- Boon: "Blessing of Akatosh"
- Event: "The Fall of Kvatch" — risk entering the burning city (take 5 damage, gain a powerful recruit) or go around
- Event: "Martin's Plea" — the heir asks for help (sacrifice a card to gain Martin Septim's buff for all creatures)
- Shop: "Imperial City Market District"
- Creature augment: "Legion Forge"
- Mini-boss: Mythic Dawn Agent (cult mage)

**Feasibility**: Card pool: **Excellent** (20+ Imperial token cards). Boss: **Ready** (Mankar Camoran is a perfect thematic legendary). Lore: **Deep** (TES IV's entire main quest).

### 2. "The Slave-Queen's Rebellion" (Alessian Revolt — Ancient Cyrodiil / Ayleid Ruins)

**Setting**: Ayleid slave pens -> White-Gold Tower exterior -> ancient battlefields -> Ayleid stronghold ruins -> Umaril's domain

**Storyline**: Journey through the echoes of the Alessian Slave Rebellion. The player descends into ancient Ayleid ruins that still hold the spirits of the fallen, facing Ayleid necromancers, their animated guardians, and ultimately the resurrected Ayleid champion Umaril the Unfeathered, who cannot truly die.

**Deck archetype**: WIL/END Spellsword — Guard-heavy Imperial deck with Drain sustain. Represents the disciplined human army that overthrew the Ayleids.

**Core cards**:
- **Ebonheart Oracle** (legendary anchor) — Drain + double health gain sustains through attrition
- **Varen Aquilarios** (legendary) — converts rune breaks to health, defensive anchor
- **Emperor Titus Mede II** (legendary) — buffs deck creatures each turn for long-game value
- **Watch Commander** — +1/+2 to all Guards
- **Kvatch Soldier** — Guard staple
- **Mentor of the Watch** — Prophecy Guard, gives Guard to hand creatures
- **Snowhawk Detachment** — grows from Guard synergy
- **Pillaging Tribune** — grants Drain
- **Cheydinhal Sapper** — Drain body
- **Bruma Profiteer** — heals on each summon
- **East Empire Crafter** — buffs 5+ health creatures with +1/+1 and Guard
- **Midnight Sweep** — Prophecy double Guard
- **Legion Zero Templar** — conditional +0/+3 Guard
- **Septim Guardsman** — 0-cost Guard body

**Enemy decks**: Ayleid ghosts, animated stone guardians, undead skeleton warriors, Ayleid necromancers

**Boss**: **Umaril the Unfeathered** (already a legendary card, END 6-cost 6/3, Guard, resurrects in other lane Silenced). Uniquely, the boss fight requires killing him twice — once in each lane — perfectly reflecting his lore as an enemy who cannot truly die.

**Node highlights**:
- Boon: "Alessia's Covenant"
- Event: "The Welkynd Chamber" — touch the ancient stones (heal 10 HP but lose a creature to a trap) or leave them
- Event: "Pelinal's Echo" — the ghost of Whitestrake offers his fury (buff a creature +3/+0 but it dies at end of next combat)
- Shop: "Ayleid Ruin Stash"
- Action augment: "Starlight Inscription" (Ayleid magic)
- Mini-boss: Ayleid Lich or Ayleid Guardian (11-cost 8/8 Guard, immune to Lethal, summons copy — already in catalog!)

**Feasibility**: Card pool: **Good** (Guard/Drain Imperial cards, 12+ core cards). Boss: **Ready** (Umaril has a unique two-phase resurrection mechanic built into his card). Lore: **Deep** (Alessian Rebellion is foundational TES lore, Pelinal Whitestrake is iconic).

### 3. "Grand Champion" (Arena Combat — Imperial City Arena)

**Setting**: Imperial City Arena district -> qualifying rounds -> champion's quarters -> the Arena floor -> Grand Champion confrontation

**Storyline**: The player enters the Imperial Arena to prove their worth. Each match is a gladiatorial bout with escalating difficulty. Between fights, the player can visit the Bloodworks for reinforcements or make deals with shady sponsors. The final challenge: the reigning Grand Champion and the mysterious Lord of the Arena.

**Deck archetype**: STR/WIL or STR-heavy — aggressive Battlemage/Crusader combat deck. Trading blows, charging in, and winning through sheer combat power.

**Core cards**:
- **Lord of the Arena** (legendary anchor) — 11-cost 8/8, makes two creatures battle. The ultimate gladiator
- **Renowned Legate** (legendary) — Breakthrough finisher
- **The Black Dragon** (legendary) — immune to Lethal, purges enemy deck on Slay
- **Imperial Siege Engine** — grows with allies
- **Fifth Legion Trainer** — buffs each summon
- **Hero of Anvil** — cost reduction on multi-summon turns
- **Gladiator Arena** (support) — damages both players each turn, aggressive tempo
- **Legion Praefect** — board-wide buff
- **Kvatch Soldier** — Guard
- **Skingrad Patroller** — efficient body
- **Imperial Legionnaire** — solid statline

**Enemy decks**: Gladiator teams (mixed races/attributes), beast fights (Minotaurs, beasts), champion duelists

**Boss**: **Lord of the Arena** (legendary, 11-cost 8/8 Minotaur). Special mechanic: forces your creatures to fight each other ("Choose two creatures to Battle"). The twist: you're not fighting the boss directly — you're fighting in the Arena while the Lord watches. The boss damages you for each creature of yours that dies.

**Node highlights**:
- Boon: "Crowd's Favor" (combat buff)
- Event: "The Bloodworks" — rest and heal, or train (lose HP, gain +1/+1 on a creature permanently)
- Event: "A Sponsor's Offer" — a wealthy patron offers gold, but you must throw a fight later (lose a future boon)
- Shop: "Arena Armorer"
- No healer nodes — the Arena is brutal
- Mini-boss: Yellow Team Champion

**Feasibility**: Card pool: **Moderate** (less mechanically cohesive — it's "fighting stuff" rather than a synergy engine). Boss: **Ready** (Lord of the Arena). Lore: **Solid** (Arena is iconic from TES IV, but shorter storyline).

### 4. "Shadows Over the Imperial City" (Thieves Guild / Intrigue — Imperial City)

**Setting**: Bravil waterfront -> Imperial City sewers -> Market District heist -> Palace infiltration -> confrontation with the Gray Fox's nemesis

**Storyline**: The Gray Fox has been betrayed. Imperial Watch agents have infiltrated the Thieves Guild, and the player must work through a web of deception, heists, and betrayals to expose the traitor and save the Guild. A storyline of stealth, cunning, and misdirection.

**Deck archetype**: AGI/INT or AGI-heavy — Pilfer/movement deck with Thieves Guild flavor. Sneak past defenses, steal value, and outmaneuver opponents.

**Core cards**:
- **Sails-Through-Storms** (legendary anchor, AGI) — Pilfer summons
- **Swims-at-Night** (legendary, AGI) — 0-cost card generation
- **Speaker Terenus** (legendary, INT) — action generation for the cunning theme
- **Abnur Tharn** (legendary, INT) — copies abilities, represents political intrigue
- **Thieves Guild Recruit** — draw + cost reduction
- Various AGI pilfer/movement cards
- **Gentleman Jim Stacey** (legendary, AGI Redguard) — steals from opponent's hand, thief flavor

**Enemy decks**: Imperial Watch guards, corrupt nobles, rival thieves

**Boss**: Custom — a traitorous Thieves Guild officer or corrupt Imperial Watch Commander. No perfect legendary boss exists for this theme.

**Node highlights**:
- Boon: "Shadow's Cover"
- Event: "The Heist" — risk/reward: attempt a theft (lose HP on fail, gain a card on success)
- Shop: "The Fence"

**Feasibility**: Card pool: **Moderate** (AGI pilfer exists but isn't specifically Imperial/Cyrodiil-themed). Boss: **Custom only**. Lore: **Solid** (Gray Fox questline is beloved).

### 5. "The King of Worms" (Necromancy — Imperial City / Ayleid Ruins)

**Setting**: Imperial City Arcane University -> Catacombs beneath the city -> Ayleid ruin necropolis -> Mannimarco's sanctum

**Storyline**: Mannimarco, the King of Worms, has returned. His necromancers are raising an army of undead beneath the Imperial City, desecrating Ayleid ruins and corrupting the Mages Guild from within. The player must root out his influence and confront the most powerful necromancer in Tamriel's history.

**Deck archetype**: END/WIL or END-heavy — Last Gasp / resurrection deck. Imperial defenders who fight, die, and come back. Reflects the thematic battle between necromancy and the Divines.

**Core cards**:
- **Mannimarco** is the BOSS, not a player card
- **Emperor Titus Mede II** (legendary anchor) — long-game value, buffs deck
- **Lucien Lachance** (legendary) — Lethal + Slay synergy
- **Caius Cosades** (legendary) — intelligence/espionage flavor
- **Black Worm Necromancer** — summons from discard (ironic — using necromancy against the necromancer)
- **Disciple of Namira** — draws cards on creature deaths
- **Doomed Adventurer** — Last Gasp buffs hand
- **Outland Patrol** — Last Gasp buffs hand
- **East Empire Crafter** — buffs high-health creatures
- **Watch Commander** — Guard synergy
- **Midnight Sweep** — Prophecy Guards

**Enemy decks**: Undead hordes (Skeletons, Zombies, Ghosts), corrupted Mages Guild members, Worm Cult necromancers

**Boss**: **Mannimarco** (already a legendary card, END 10-cost 6/6, summons a creature from opponent's discard). Special mechanic: "Each turn, Mannimarco summons a creature from your discard pile to fight for him." Your dead creatures become his army — a deeply thematic and mechanically unique boss fight.

**Node highlights**:
- Boon: "Divine Ward" (protection against necromancy)
- Event: "The Corrupted Scholar" — a Mages Guild member offers forbidden knowledge (gain an action but lose a creature)
- Event: "Ayleid Catacombs" — choose: quick path through undead (combat) or slow path through traps (lose HP)
- Shop: "Arcane University Stores"
- Creature augment: "Holy Enchantment" (anti-undead buff)
- Mini-boss: Worm Cult Archlich

**Feasibility**: Card pool: **Good** (END Imperials + Last Gasp cards). Boss: **Ready** (Mannimarco is perfectly designed — his mechanic of stealing from your discard makes for a narratively perfect fight). Lore: **Deep** (Mannimarco appears across multiple TES games).

## Recommendation

**Build "The Gates of Oblivion" first.** It has the strongest combination across all criteria:

1. **Card pool depth** — The Imperial token army is the deepest card pool in the entire catalog. 20+ cards with direct summon/buff synergy, all in WIL/END. General Tullius, Legate Rikke, Fifth Legion Trainer, Imperial Reinforcements, Legion Praefect, and Praetorian Commander create a clear "summon tokens, buff them, overwhelm" gameplay identity.

2. **Boss quality** — Mankar Camoran already exists as a legendary with the Invade mechanic. His "Invade on every action + buff Oblivion Gates" creates a race condition: the player must kill him before his Oblivion Gates scale out of control. This is thematically perfect and mechanically engaging.

3. **Lore iconicness** — The Oblivion Crisis is arguably the most recognizable TES storyline after Skyrim's dragon crisis. Kvatch's fall, the Oblivion Gates, Martin Septim's sacrifice — these are deeply embedded in player memory.

4. **Mechanical uniqueness** — No existing adventure uses a token army deck. The WIL token-summon identity (flooding the board with cheap Grunts and buffing them) plays completely differently from the STR/INT item decks, AGI assassin decks, and END ramp decks in other regions.

**Second priority**: "The King of Worms" — Mannimarco's boss mechanic (summoning your dead creatures against you) is one of the most unique boss designs possible, and the END Last Gasp deck has a distinct identity. However, the deck is slightly less mechanically cohesive than the Legion token army.

**Third priority**: "The Slave-Queen's Rebellion" — Umaril's two-phase resurrection boss is brilliant and built into his existing card, but the Guard/Drain deck might feel slower and less exciting in practice.
